Main English Skills

There are four main language skills students must master:

1. Listening: The ability to understand spoken instructions or conversations.
Example:
A: Good morning, everyone. Please prepare your reports.
B: Yes, Sir.

Tips:
- Focus on important keywords.
- Pay attention to context and intonation.
- Practice listening to English audios regularly.

2. Speaking: The ability to express ideas, opinions, or information orally.
Common Expressions:
Greeting: Good morning, how are you today?.
Introducing: Let me introduce myself. My name is Rina.
Asking for help: Could you help me, please?
Giving direction: Go straight and turn right.
Offering help: May I help you?
Expressing opinion: I think learning English is very important.

Tips:
- Speak naturally with the correct intonation.
- Use simple sentences.
- Practice speaking in front of a mirror or with friends.

3. Reading: The ability to understand the meaning of written texts.
Types of texts commonly studied:
Descriptive Text: Describes a person, place, or thing.
Example: My school is big and clean.
Recount Text: Tells about past experiences.
Example: Last weekend I went to the beach.
Procedure Text: Explains how to do something.
Example: How to make a cup of tea.
Announcement: Gives official information.
Example: There will be a meeting at 9 a.m.
Notice: Gives a warning or instruction.
Example: Don’t litter!
Invitation: Invites someone to an event.
Example: You are invited to my birthday party.

2. Writing: The ability to write sentences or paragraphs using correct grammar and structure.
Common Expressions:

Examples of Writing Forms:
Introducing: Let me introduce myself. My name is Rina.
Descriptive Paragraph:
My classroom is large and clean. There are 20 chairs and a whiteboard.
Recount Text:
Yesterday, I visited my grandmother. She cooked my favorite food.
Procedure Text:
1. Procedure Text:
2. Open Microsoft Word.
3. Start typing your document.
Application Letter:
Dear Manager,
I am writing to apply for the position of receptionist. Sincerely,
Dinda

Tips:
- Use correct grammar and punctuation.
- Organize sentences into clear paragraphs.

Grammar (Structure of the Language)

Grammar is the rule that forms meaningful and correct sentences.

Commonly used tenses and examples:

Simple Present Tense: She works in a bank. (Habitual action)

Present Continuous Tense: They are studying now. (Action happening now)

Simple Past Tense: I visited Bali last year. (Action in the past)

Simple Future Tense: I will go to school tomorrow. (Future action)

Present Perfect Tense: She has finished her homework. (Action that has been completed)

Vocabulary

Vocabulary refers to the collection of words used to express ideas.
In SMK, vocabulary learning is adjusted to the students’ field of expertise.

Examples of Common Vocabulary by Theme:
School: student, teacher, subject, exam
Office: meeting, document, schedule, report
Hotel & Tourism: guest, reservation, luggage, receptionist
IT & Computer: software, network, database, program
Factory: machine, worker, production, safety

Functional Texts

Functional texts are short texts used in daily or workplace communication.

Examples:
1. Announcement:
“The meeting will be held on Friday at 9 a.m.”
2. Notice:
“Keep the room clean!”
3. Invitation:
“You are invited to our farewell party.”
4. Advertisement:
“Wanted: Cashier with good English skill.”
5. Warning:
“Danger! High voltage.”

Communication Etiquette in English

1. Use polite expressions such as please, thank you, and excuse me.

2. Listen carefully to your conversation partner.
3. Do not interrupt when someone is speaking.
4. Use friendly facial expressions and tone of voice.
5. Avoid using slang or rude words in formal situations.
